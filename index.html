Threading of PGL2-webs

Geometry and Topology REU at UCLA. With Vijay Higgins, Bryan Silva, Amy Somers, David Stephens, and Parth Wokhlu. In preparation.

Abstract

We study webs on surfaces for the PGL2 and SL2 skein theories specialized at roots of unity. We construct the PGL2 analogue of Chebyshev polynomials and use them to construct central elements in the skein algebra.

We then focus on the cases of the 3-punctured sphere and the closed torus. We compute the Frobenius homomorphism for these two surfaces and show that the PGL2 Frobenius homomorphism for the torus is not injective when q2 is a root of unity of order n with n even.

We give a conjectural formula for the Frobenius image of a particular case of a theta web on the 3-punctured sphere, and verify the formula for all roots of unity q2 of order n < 6.

Construction of the Alexander Invariant

University of Chicago REU, mentored by Peter May and Irving Dai.

Abstract

This paper motivates and details the construction of the Alexander invariant, a powerful invariant in knot theory. It assumes familiarity with algebraic topology at the level of the fundamental group and homology theory.

Markov Chains and Musical Composition

University of Chicago REU, mentored by Philip Lo.

Abstract

This paper provides a brief overview of discrete Markov chains, particularly concerning their long-time behavior, primarily following Greg Lawler's Introduction to Stochastic Processes. It then studies applications of this theory to stochastic music composition.
